💡 Eco Tips

Shop, wash & care
the green way

Practical, evidence-based tips to slash your fashion footprint without sacrificing style.

Tip 01 ♻️
Buy Second-Hand First
Thrifting is the single most impactful change you can make. Buying a used item creates zero new demand for resources. Look for vintage, charity shops, or platforms like Vinted and Depop.
⬇️ Up to 82% less CO₂
Tip 02 🔎
Check Fibre Composition
Always read the label. Aim for single-material garments (100% cotton, 100% linen) — they're easier to recycle and repair. Blended fabrics often end up in landfill.
♻️ Easier recycling
Tip 03 🌿
Choose Certified Organic
GOTS-certified organic cotton uses 91% less water and 62% less energy than conventional cotton. Look for the GOTS, OEKO-TEX, or Fair Trade logo on the tag.
💧 91% less water
Tip 04 📦
Avoid Fast Fashion Hauls
The fast fashion industry produces 92 million tonnes of textile waste per year. Instead, follow a "cost-per-wear" mindset — invest in fewer, better quality pieces that last years.
🗑️ Reduces waste
Tip 05 📍
Buy Local When Possible
Locally made clothing can cut transport emissions by up to 80%. Support local designers and makers — your purchase also supports local economies and fair wages.
✈️ ↓80% transport CO₂
Tip 06 🧵
Learn Basic Repairs
Extending a garment's life by just 9 months reduces its carbon, water, and waste footprint by 20–30%. A simple YouTube tutorial on sewing can save dozens of items from landfill.
📉 20–30% less impact
Tip 07 🤝
Swap & Borrow
Clothing swaps with friends or local communities let you refresh your wardrobe with zero environmental cost. Apps like Nuw and By Rotation let you rent occasion wear instead of buying.
🌍 Zero new resources
Tip 08 📊
Use EcoFit Before Buying
Before adding a new item to your cart, use our Impact Calculator to check its environmental score. Compare materials and choose the lower-impact option — it takes under 60 seconds.
🔍 Informed decisions

🧺 Washing & Care Guide

Up to 60% of a garment's lifetime carbon footprint comes from how you wash and dry it. These changes make a real difference.

🌡️
Wash at 30°C
-57%
Switching from 60°C to 30°C cuts energy use by 57% with no loss in cleaning effectiveness for normal loads.
🌬️
Line Dry
-100%
Tumble dryers are one of the biggest household energy users. Air drying costs nothing and is gentler on fabrics.
👗
Full Loads Only
-30%
Running a half-empty machine wastes water and energy. Wait for a full load to maximise every wash cycle.
🧴
Eco Detergent
-20%
Plant-based concentrated detergents reduce chemical pollution and often come in plastic-free packaging.
🐟
Microplastic Bag
90%↓
Use a Guppyfriend bag or Cora Ball to catch microplastics shed by synthetic fabrics before they reach waterways.
👃
Spot Clean First
-100%
For small stains or light wear, spot cleaning with a damp cloth avoids a full wash cycle entirely — saving huge amounts of water.
🔢
Wash Less Often
-50%
Jeans last much longer washed every 10 wears. Most tops are fine for 2–3 wears before washing. Jumpers can go 5+ wears.
🗂️
Read Care Labels
Extended life
Following care instructions can double or triple a garment's lifespan. Hot washing delicates destroys fibres and shortens life dramatically.

🏷️ Certification Guide

Not all eco labels are equal. Here's what each certification actually guarantees — and what to watch out for.

🌿
GOTS
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Highest Trust
Global Organic Textile Standard. The most rigorous certification covering the entire supply chain from raw fibre to finished product. Prohibits harmful chemicals and requires fair labour conditions.
Organic FibresChemical SafetyFair LabourTraceabilityWater Treatment
🏷️
OEKO-TEX® Standard 100
⭐⭐⭐⭐ High Trust
Tests every component of a garment (fabric, buttons, zips) for over 100 harmful substances. Primarily a product safety standard — does not guarantee environmental manufacturing standards.
Chemical TestingProduct SafetyNo Greenwashing
🤝
Fair Trade
⭐⭐⭐⭐ High Trust
Ensures fair wages and safe working conditions for farmers and garment workers. Includes a community premium paid to workers. Focuses on social impact over environmental impact.
Fair WagesSafe ConditionsCommunity PremiumNo Child Labour
🅱️
B Corp
⭐⭐⭐⭐ High Trust
Certifies entire companies (not products) for social and environmental performance, accountability, and transparency. Covers governance, workers, community, environment, and customers.
Company-wideSocial ImpactEnvironmentalTransparency
💧
bluesign®
⭐⭐⭐⭐ High Trust
Focuses on fabric mills — requiring safe chemicals, clean water and air treatment, and worker safety. Particularly strong for reducing harmful chemical use in dyeing and finishing processes.
Chemical SafetyWater ManagementAir EmissionsWorker Safety
⚠️
Watch Out For...
⭐ Greenwashing Risk
Vague terms like "eco-friendly," "sustainable," "natural," or "green" have no legal definition and no third-party verification. Always look for a specific, named third-party certification with a logo.
❌ "Eco-friendly" ❌ "Sustainable" ❌ "Natural" ❌ "Green"
